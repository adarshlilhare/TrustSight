Support Center

Frequently asked questions about TrustSight Browser Guard.

What does TrustSight do? +
TrustSight is a browser security extension that blocks malware, phishing, scams, ads, and trackers in real-time. It uses an Edge AI engine that runs 100% on your device — your browsing data never leaves your computer.
How does Pre-Navigation Blocking work? +
When you click a link or type a URL, TrustSight intercepts the request before the page even begins to load. It checks the domain against a local threat database containing 150+ known malicious sites plus pattern-matching for suspicious domains. If a threat is detected, it redirects you to a warning page instead.
What is the Allow List? +
The Allow List lets you whitelist specific domains that you trust. If TrustSight blocks a site you know is safe, you can either click "Continue Anyway" on the block page (it auto-adds to the Allow List) or manually add it via the Dashboard. Allowed domains bypass the threat scanner.
How are ads and trackers blocked? +
TrustSight injects a content script into every webpage. It uses CSS selectors to identify common ad containers (Google Ads, ad iframes, popups) and hides them. It also detects tracker scripts from known domains (Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, etc.) and removes them from the DOM entirely. A MutationObserver continuously watches for new elements loading in.
What does "Partially Protected" mean? +
If you turn off one or more protection toggles (Malware, Scam, Ads, PUPs) but keep at least one enabled, TrustSight shows "Partially Protected" in amber. If all are off, it shows "Protection is OFF". On system/empty pages (like chrome:// URLs), it shows "Empty page" since extensions cannot scan browser internals.
Is my browsing data sent anywhere? +
No. TrustSight runs entirely offline. The threat database, AI scanning engine, and all settings are stored locally in chrome.storage.local. Your browsing data, history, and URLs never leave your device.
How do I report a missed threat? +
Go to Support → Report a Missed Threat. Enter the suspicious URL, select a category, and optionally add a description. Your report will be sent directly to our security team via email for analysis and inclusion in future threat database updates.
